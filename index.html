Naive Bayes Hotel Review Classifier

I wrote a Naive Bayes classifier from scratch (without the use of any ML libraries) to classify hotel reviews by two separate labels. The first label classifies the reviews as having either positive or negative sentiment and the second classifies the reviews as being either truthful or deceptive. This classifier returned a mean F1 score of 0.88.

US Wildfires Geo-Visualization

According to the PNAS (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America), forest fires in the western United States have been increasing in both size and severity for several decades. These increases have been attributed to human activities and climate change. With all of the recent fires the US has been experiencing, this information is top of mind for health authorities, fire departments, environmental organizations, and even the common person such as you or me.

The goal of this project was to first provide users with an understanding of the recent growth in US wildfire incidence and severity. Secondly, we promote action over reaction as it relates to fighting wildfires. Historic patterns and relationships among wildfire causes, seasonality, location, and spending support the notion that being proactive towards preventing fires is achievable. This approach of working to prevent wildfires should be given just as much weight and attention as is given to putting out fires that have already started.

We utilized html, javascript, and d3.js in conjunction with Vue.app in order to bring this interactive visualization together.

A video walkthrough of the application can be found here.

MSCI Emerging Markets Hit Rate Study

Goal:
The goal of this project was to determine which emerging market countries have historically (over the past 10 years) experienced a higher percentage of companies with 3-year returns greater than the return of the MSCI Emerging Markets benchmark.

Definitions:
Hit Rate - The percentage of companies that outperformed the MSCI Emerging Markets benchmark over a 3-year period.
Winner - A company that outperforms the MSCI Emerging Markets benchmark.

Method:

  1. Utilized Bloomberg EQS screen to pull return data for all companies of specific market cap within Emerging Markets countries as defined by MSCI
  2. Leveraged Tableau for data visualization


Key Takeaways:
  • China, Taiwan, India, Hong Kong, and Thailand made up 79% of winners over the entire 10 year period. These top 5 countries have also displayed higher average hit rates than our universe’s average
  • These top 5 countries have also displayed higher average hit rates than our universe’s average


Possible Actions:
Because 79% of winning companies have come from 5 of the ~25 Emerging Markets countries, there is evidence to support a move toward narrowing the the focus of a portfolio from an asset management standpoint. As time to visit and discover companies is limited, focusing on a select number of countries with higher historic success/opportunity could prove beneficial.

National Parks & Rock Climbing Recommender System

I’ve been passionate about the outdoors for as long as I can remember. Growing up in Colorado, followed by living in Utah for 4.5 years prior to coming to USC for grad school, served as somewhat of an incubator for falling in love with nature - it allowed me to become an avid hiker, climber, and canyoneer. I started rock climbing with my Father and 3 brothers when I was around 9 years old and have been looking for any excuse to get outside to climb ever since. Over the course of my life, I’ve enjoyed visiting a number of the national parks, monuments, and historic sites maintained by the U.S. National Park Service. Rocky Mountain National Park, Zion National Park, Dinosaur National Monument, and Arches National Park are just a few of my favorites. Whenever I plan to visit a National Park/Monument with friends or family, I always like to find out if there is quality rock climbing nearby, to complement a day of visiting the park. I’ve found that it takes a bit of navigating online to find all of the information I need. I will have to navigate to the U.S. National Park Service website to find information about park hours, events, and alerts – this is especially helpful to make sure specific trails or parts of the park aren’t shut down for maintenance or road repair. I will then have to look up the weather forecast to make sure it is worth going to the park. As far as rock climbing is concerned, I will have to navigate to Mountain Project and then struggle my way through locating climbing areas nearby the park I plan to visit. Finally, I will have to use google maps to see if the climbs that look interesting are within a reasonable distance of the park. Now that I’ve provided some context, the aim of this project was to leverage python3 and a number of APIs in conjunction with some webscraping to bring all of the aforementioned data together in just a few simple strokes of the keyboard.

I utilized python to access 2 APIs and scrape 2 websites in order to provide users with recommendations of top-rated climbing routes within the desired radius of their national park selection. Recommended routes are displayed on a google map with info boxes that provide weather forecasts for the remainder of the week, national park advisories, recommendations for activities to consider in the park, climbing route difficulty/popularity, and links to all sources.
